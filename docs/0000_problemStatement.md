# Problem Statement

## The Core Problem

Modern users create accounts across dozens of services using Google, Apple, and email-based signups. Over time, many of those accounts become inactive, forgotten, or abandoned, yet they still retain personal data.

This creates a visibility problem:

- users do not know which services still have their data
- stale accounts remain linked to active inboxes
- dormant accounts increase long-term privacy risk
- users rarely have the time or motivation to audit those services manually

## Why This Matters

Forgotten accounts are not harmless. They can still hold:

- email addresses
- profile data
- billing details
- usage history
- linked recovery methods

If those services are breached, inactive users may not even remember they had an account there.

## User Pain Point

The current cleanup process is broken because it is:

- manual
- slow
- repetitive
- easy to postpone

As a result, most users never clean up their digital footprint unless a tool makes the process visible and actionable.

## GhostGuard's Goal

GhostGuard exists to help users:

1. identify likely services still tied to their inbox
2. find dormant or ghost accounts
3. highlight breached or risky domains
4. draft deletion requests for manual review and action
