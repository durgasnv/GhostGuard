# Future Scope

## Near-Term Improvements

These are the most practical next steps for strengthening the MVP.

### 1. Full Gmail Pagination

GhostGuard should scan more than a small recent slice of the inbox.

This would improve:

- coverage
- confidence
- ghost-account discovery quality

### 2. Better Error Visibility

Users should see clearer runtime feedback when:

- OAuth fails
- the backend is offline
- Gmail metadata requests are blocked
- no external domains are found

### 3. Improved Domain Intelligence

Service detection can become more accurate through:

- better parsing rules
- domain-to-service mapping
- filtering of newsletters and noisy senders
- confidence scoring

### 4. Better Breach Data

GhostGuard should integrate stronger breach intelligence with:

- more coverage
- date information
- severity or priority ranking

## Medium-Term Product Expansion

### 1. Review and Ignore Controls

Users should be able to:

- mark false positives
- hide irrelevant services
- confirm important services

This would improve trust and personalization.

### 2. Smarter Cleanup Workflow

The deletion flow can be improved with:

- mailto launch support
- company-specific contact suggestions
- reusable draft templates
- lightweight session tracking of reviewed services

### 3. Better Reporting

GhostGuard could generate:

- audit summaries
- cleanup reports
- risk snapshots

This would increase value for both consumers and teams.

## Long-Term Commercial Direction

### 1. Privacy Operations Platform

GhostGuard could evolve from a single-user cleanup tool into a privacy operations product.

That could include:

- repeated privacy audits
- exposure trend tracking
- policy-driven cleanup workflows

### 2. Team and Enterprise Use Cases

Potential long-term business expansion:

- employee offboarding cleanup
- stale SaaS account discovery
- identity exposure reduction
- security and compliance support workflows

### 3. Multi-Provider Expansion

Future versions could extend beyond Gmail to support:

- Outlook
- Microsoft 365
- other metadata-compatible email ecosystems

### 4. Privacy Concierge Layer

GhostGuard could eventually support a higher-touch assisted model for users who want guided cleanup instead of self-service only.

## Strategic Direction

The strongest future version of GhostGuard is not just an inbox scanner.

It is a broader privacy cleanup and exposure reduction workflow.

## Summary

The next stage of GhostGuard should focus on:

- deeper and more accurate scanning
- better user trust and transparency
- stronger risk intelligence
- easier cleanup execution
- eventual expansion into repeat privacy operations
